Ladakh trekking

About trekking in Ladakh

Only an hour’s flight from Delhi, Ladakh looks like nowhere else on earth. Turquoise lakes reflect snow-dusted peaks. Monasteries tower like beehives over the stark, sun-drenched hillside. The high-altitude desert, home to a welcoming Buddhist and Muslim population, is one of India’s safest and most spectacular regions. And it is best explored on foot. Here are some of the best treks in Ladakh to choose from.

Helpful Tips
  • Most trekking routes are open from April to October, although higher-altitude treks have shorter windows. (This does not include winter trails like the Chadar frozen river trek or snow-leopard spotting.)
  • Ladakh has a sensitive eco-system and a limited water supply, so be mindful of your footprint. Use refillable thermoses, avoid flush toilets, and don’t leave any garbage behind
  • Prepare for strong rays: bring sunscreen and protective clothing!
  • Permits are needed for many areas, including Nubra Valley and Tso Moriri. They must be obtained from travel agents

Popular Trekking in Ladakh

Markha Valley trek

Being one of the most fascinating treks of Ladakh, the Markha Valley Winter Trek takes you on a beautiful journey to the cold and dark yet absolutely pretty Markha Valley with colorful prayer flags, mani walls, distinctive villages, monasteries, high passes and exotic wildlife.

Stok Kangri trek

Stok Kangri is a favorite among trekking enthusiasts who wish to take it up a notch by summiting one of the world’s highest trekkable peaks.

Kang Yatse 2 trek

Kang Yatse 2 is a throne shaped peak that is a healthy contender to Stok Kangri. Located in the Markha Valley, the Kang Yatse 2 trek takes you through culturally rich villages, mani walls, old monasteries, and ruined forts.

Digar La trek

A wonderful bike ride to Leh-Ladakh, a land covered with snow-capped mountains and the highest motorable passes, makes you feel at the top of the world.

Chadar Trek

Undeniably the most famous trek of Ladakh, the Chadar Trek lets you walk on a sheet of ice formed on the frozen Zanskar River.

Sham Valley trek

Known as the “Baby Trek” of Ladakh, the Sham Valley trek takes you to Sham Valley, the lower part of Ladakh. This trek can be done throughout the Ladakh and is a great way to experience the history and culture of Ladakh.
