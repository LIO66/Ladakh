About Nubra Valley Tourism

The breathtaking Nubra Valley’s major attraction is its large cold desert near Hunder Village. Encircled by the lofty jagged barren mountains, the cold desert is where one can enjoy the most popular activities in Nubra, Bactrian Camel (Double-Humped) Ride. Camping in Hunder is also one of the must-do activities in Nubra. For those who love adventure, a bit too much, can ride across Nubra on motorbike or mountain bikes for that adrenaline rush. On the journey, enjoy the view of some of the oldest monasteries in Ladakh like Diskit and Samstanling. The 32m Jampa (Maitreya) Buddha statue is also one of the top attractions in Nubra that sits facing Pakistan and overlooking the meandering Shyok River.

The remote villages of Sumur, Panamik, and Turtuk have also become quite popular amongst travellers who wish to learn about the culture and diversity of Ladakh. Panamik is popular for its hot water spring and a sacred Yarab Tso (lake) is also a must-see here. Turtuk, on the other hand peeks the interest of those who want a closer look at the tribes of Ladakh and enjoy a different landscape in the region. Eco-camping is one of the major attractions in Turtuk, the last inhabited village on Indo-Pak Border.

Best Time to Visit Nubra

Summer Season In nubra

In Nubra, between March and June, temperature ranges from -1°C to 15°C. The valley is covered under the blanket of snow, which only starts to melt by the month of May.

Monsoon Season In nubra

The months between July and September are considered the best time to visit Nubra. The temperature is between 15°C and 35°C, and since Nubra is situated at a high altitude, it receives very little rain.

Winter Season In nubra

From the month of October, Nubra starts to receive snow and the mercury dips to 0°C and even less. If you enjoy snow, winter is the best time to visit Nubra Valley.
